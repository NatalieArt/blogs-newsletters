2025: The Year Sogni Came Alive.

From 1 million to 150 million creations, $4M raised, and a community-owned AI toolkit running on 1,000+ GPUs, this issue is both a year-in-review and a roadmap into 2026.

The theme

If 2025 proved the Supernet works, 2026 is framed as the year creators, developers, and community tools catch up with that infrastructure.

Year in review

2025 was the year the Supernet proved it could work at scale.

The issue leads with the three headline numbers: 150 million creations, 85,000 artists, and 1,000+ GPUs. It treats those not as vanity metrics, but as the proof that a community-owned creative AI toolkit can operate across a serious infrastructure base.

That is why the newsletter links readers straight into the deeper wrap-up at 2025.sogni.ai. Volume 18 is less about reliving the year and more about setting direction for what the next phase should unlock.

Road ahead

“If 2025 was about proving the Supernet works, 2026 is about seeing what you do with it.”

The newsletter makes a deliberate pivot from infrastructure validation into product, community, and developer leverage.

2026: What’s next

Full video, everywhere.

The first roadmap pillar is straightforward: video expands across all Sogni apps, so people can move between image, video, and audio without switching toolchains. The article specifically points toward video transition, motion transfer, music-synced edits, longer clips, and more refined controls.

More consistent workflowsImage, video, and audio stop feeling like separate products and start feeling like one connected stack.
Longer, richer clipsVideo features are framed not as experiments, but as tools creators will actually want to use repeatedly.
Community

Profiles, social, and discovery turn the network outward.

The second pillar is about making the community more legible. Artist profiles become a home base for work, style, and links. Discovery surfaces bring following, exploration, and real-time visibility to what other creators are making.

The most interesting detail is the framing around remix culture: prompts, settings, and workflows become shareable enough that “How did you make that?” can become “Here, remix this.”

Developers and models

SDK upgrades, better starter apps, and tighter model integration push the ecosystem forward.

The issue also points to a stronger developer story: a more usable vibe-coding starter app with auth and payments already wired, better docs, and more examples. That matters because it lowers the friction for anyone building on top of the Supernet instead of just consuming the apps that already exist.

SDK

More complete scaffolding so developers can focus on the product layer faster.

Docs

Better examples and clearer onboarding around how to build on the network.

Models

New models and stronger LLM integration point toward a broader creative stack in 2026.

Next move

Read this edition as a roadmap, not just a recap.

Volume 18 matters because it tells you what the team thought the next year should optimize for: richer media workflows, stronger creator identity, and lower-friction building for developers. It is the context piece that makes the later feature-heavy issues easier to read.
