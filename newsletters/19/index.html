Season 6 is live.

New video tools, new camera controls, new mixer features, and a 10 million SOGNI Season 6 kickoff. January was a monster month, and this edition breaks down the big pieces that shipped.

Quick note

“January was a monster month.” This issue reads like a full capability expansion rather than a single feature drop, especially for image-to-video creators.

Edition intro

A stack of upgrades landed all at once.

This edition opens with a simple premise: the Supernet upgraded, Season 6 kicked off with a 10 million SOGNI prize pool, and a wave of new video and image tools changed how people create inside Sogni.

Instead of treating those tools as isolated launches, the newsletter frames them as a broader shift toward richer motion workflows and more creator control.

Your new video superpowers

Motion Transfer, Sound to Video, and Change Perspective push image workflows further into motion.

Motion TransferAnimate-Move lets your character inherit movement from a source video, while Animate-Replace keeps the motion and swaps the subject. The issue also calls out direct Mac webcam capture as part of the workflow.
Sound to VideoDrop in audio, let the tool analyze rhythm and dynamics, and generate movement that matches the track. The built-in mic flow makes live audio capture part of the same loop.
Change PerspectiveRe-render the same image from new camera angles while keeping subject and style consistent. Distance, orientation, and elevation are all part of the control set.

The throughline is obvious: Sogni is turning single-image workflows into multi-angle, multi-motion workflows without asking creators to leave the same ecosystem.

Editing upgrades

Clip Mixer now behaves much more like a real editorial tool.

Volume 19 highlights three changes that matter immediately for longer sequences: trimming while keeping the last frame, mute/unmute per clip, and an additional audio track that can run across the full sequence.

Trim / Keep last frame

Helps prevent repeated boundary frames and makes transitions between clips cleaner.

Clip audio control

Mute or unmute individual clips when you need to keep only the pieces that serve the final sequence.

Export modes

Fast Combine, High Quality, and Share-safe give you speed, polish, or compatibility depending on the delivery target.

Camera workflows

Photobooth and Sogni 360 both expand the multi-angle mindset.

The newsletter calls out the new 3D Camera Angle feature in Photobooth, with up to 96 perspectives from a single image once you start varying distance, height, and rotation. It also points people toward multi-angle presets, local project reuse, and batch transition workflows.

Alongside that, Sogni 360 gets orbit-video momentum, reinforcing the bigger theme of the issue: creators are no longer locked into one hero frame.

Season 6

All of these features landed into a bigger competitive moment.

Season 6 did not launch as a quiet iteration. It launched with a major prize pool, a stronger Supernet base, and a set of tools that makes video feel central rather than optional.

What to do next

Use this edition as a checklist for what changed in the product.

If you have not explored the January wave yet, the practical order is straightforward: try Motion Transfer, test Sound to Video, reframe a still with Change Perspective, then move into Clip Mixer to finish with better editorial control.
